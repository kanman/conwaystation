About us

We are proudly one of Australia’s best cattle stations

Conways Station is 139,200 hectares in size, situated 180 kilometres from Katherine and 570 kilometres from Darwin. The property borders Arnhem land and Kakadu National Park and features black soil plains, red Tippera Soils, escarpment and lots of water springs and rivers. The mix of sweeping escarpments and waterfalls, billabongs and plains makes this country splendid and beautifully picturesque.

More Info


Cattle

Conways Station is well established for cattle with around 5000 head on hand. Consisting over 3000 breeders, the station turns off up to 1,500 head per year for a mix of live export and domestic market.


Buffalo

The station has a large population of wild buffalo which are professionally mustered and sold into a mix of the domestic and export market.


Carbon Farming Initiative

Conways participates in the Carbon Farming Initiative (CFI), with proactive farming practices recognised under the CFI to reduce emissions.


New Projects ahead

Conways Station is planning further farm improvements, utilizing what mother nature has offered to us. We are in the planning stage of some very exciting projects, more to come in the next few months. With an average rainfall of over 1000mm per annum, creeks and rivers running across the property, the water situation is promising for cattle and plantations, Conway’s has a character of its own.
